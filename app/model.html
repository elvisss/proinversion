PPP: SHARING PROGRESS

REDUCING THE GAP

The infrastructure gap in Peru amounts to US$ 159 billion. The Government’s goal is, at least, to halve it through medium- and long-term investments for more than US$ 70 billion. But this effort will not be carried out only with public funds, so that a key piece will be to promote the participation of the private sector by means of the public-private partnerships scheme (PPP).

PPP IN PERU

Since the mid-90s, Peru promotes the implementation of public infrastructure and services projects. Initially, it was through concessions and, from last decade, through public-private partnerships (PPP), which are managed within an ad-hoc and stable legal framework that is based on the principles of transparency, competitiveness and predictability.

  • The purpose of PPP is to incorporate the financing, experience, know-how and technology of the private sector into public works.
  • PPP agreements are designed to provide an efficient distribution of risks and resources.
  • PPP are mainly carried out by means of concessions granted by the State.
  • Since 2000, Peru has an agency specialized in promoting PPP projects, which has allowed the accumulation of expertise. That entity is ProInversión.
  • PPP projects can emerge by either the State (public initiatives) or the private sector (unsolicited proposals).

Modalities

  • Self-sustaining: Projects that will generate the necessary income to recover the investment and to cover the operation and maintenance expenses. They are financed by the fees, prices or tolls that users pay, so that they do not need the use of public funds as joint financing.
  • Co-financed: Projects that demand resources in cash from the State or need financial and/or non-financial guarantees, to become attractive to the private sector. The limit of resources or government guarantees to deem a PPP project as co-financed is 5% of the entire investment.

Basic features

The conduction of the PPP processes is based on the following principles:

  • Levels of service: Establishment of minimum quality standards that the concessionaire must fulfill. For example, surface roughness in highways or waiting time of ships in ports, among others.
  • Economic-financial balance: Restoration of the economic-financial balance of the authorization in case the State dictates legislation that affects the concessionaire’s income or costs.
  • Dispute settlement: National or international arbitration, according to the amount subject of the dispute.
  • Adequate risk sharing: Risks are allocated to the party with the highest capability of managing them, seeking the mitigation of their impact on the infrastructure availability and/or service quality.
  • Interaction with the market: Remarks and suggestions are received to be included into the bases of the biddings and/or concession agreements.
  • Social and environmental license.
  • Transparency: Free Access to information on the situation of biddings.
  • Suitable qualification of bidders: During the process, technical, legal and financial requisites are established for the selection of companies or consortiums that take part in the biddings.

UNSOLICITED PROPOSALS

The private sector is able to submit proposals of projects that can be carried out throughout PPP.

  • Proposals remain confidential and reserved since their submission up until their declaration of interest.
  • The State is not bound to issue any pronouncement regarding the submitted unsolicited proposals.
  • Unsolicited proposals are subject to evaluation by the State.
  • The State can require adjustments to the proposals during their evaluation.
  • When the State deems that the proposal fits its objectives, there begins a diffusion process seeking to promote the interest of third parties for competing with the original proposal.

Benefits

  • The proposer has the right to match the winning economic bid in the cases that the unsolicited proposal was admitted to be awarded by concession.
  • Refund of the expenses incurred in the preparation of the studies underpinning the proposal.

FLAGSHIP PROJECTS

Between 2000 and September 2016, 113 PPP projects have been carried out or granted by the Peruvian State, which emerged from both public and unsolicited proposals. Said projects entail estimated investments of US$ 30.9 billion.

  • Amongst others, the highlights of the scheme have been the following: the international airports Jorge Chávez (Lima) and Chinchero (Cusco), as well as 17 regional airports, 5 port terminals, 12 road projects, 3 irrigation projects, Line 2 of the Lima Metro, the South-Peruvian Gas Pipeline, the Kuélap cableway (Amazonas), 2 wastewater treatment plants, 19 electric transmission lines and several telecom projects (4G band, rural broadband and the national optical fiber backbone network).
