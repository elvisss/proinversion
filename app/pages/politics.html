INVEST IN PERU

INVESTORS ARE WELCOME

ON THE RIGHT TRACK

In Peru, the private sector is the leading driver of the economy. The economic model adopted by the country a quarter of a century ago has one of its pillars in the promotion of private investment, both national and foreign, offering it a legal framework that grants an egalitarian treatment regardless its origin, as well as opportunities in all the productive and services activities, within a free market approach.

GENERAL FRAMEWORK

The Constitution enshrines the freedom of the private initiative. The role of the State is to foster the development of the country and to act in the promotion of employment, health, education, safety, public services and infrastructure. In contrast to other countries, the Peruvian legal framework promotes foreign investment, since it is based on the principle of national treatment, therefore establishing the same rights and obligations than those for local investors. Also, the Peruvian State guarantees:

  • The right to open competition and private property.
  • Freedom to purchase shares of national companies.
  • Freedom to obtain internal and external funding.
  • Freedom to remit royalties and profits from investments.
  • Ease to perform operations in local and foreign currency, without any exchange rate control.
  • Access to international mechanisms of dispute settlement.
  • Non-existence of legal requirements on performance compliance.
  • Investors are not required to pass any authorization process prior to their operation in the country.
  • Foreign investors can participate in all economic activities. They only require special permits for investments within 50 Km from the national borderline, and in those related to weapons, ammunition and explosives.
  • Also, foreign investments in maritime cabotage only require a national majority shareholder; and in air transport, a maximum 49% of foreign capital is permitted at the beginning of operations which can be increased to up to 70% after 6 months of operations.

Investment climate

Peru ranks among the three South American countries with the best economic performance, according to assessments conducted by rating agencies, multilateral organisms and international think tanks.

  • Peru’s macroeconomic fundamentals (low inflation, managed public debt, accumulation of international reserves, etc.) have made possible that, since 2009, the main credit rating agencies rate as investment grade the debt issued by the country.
  • Peru ranks third in South America and sixth in Latin America in the Global Competitiveness Index (GCI) of the World Economic Forum. Compared to the 2015-2016 report, Peru improved in the indicators related to macroeconomic environment, market efficiency and innovation in the 2016-2017 report.
  • Regarding the easy to doing business, Peru ranks third in Latin America in the Word Bank’s Doing Business report, only behind Mexico and Colombia.
  • Among South American countries, Peru outstands in registering property, paying taxes, getting credit, trading across borders, dealing with construction permits and getting electricity.

PROMOTION AND PROTECTION OF INVESTMENTS

Since Peru liberalized its market, it has put special attention in the implementation of tools aimed to strengthen the investments climate. Included among them are the international investment agreements and those to avoid double taxation, as well as the access to international mechanisms of dispute settlement.

Likewise, Peru has two special regimes oriented to provide assurance and to generate favorable financial conditions for new enterprises. Said regimes are the legal stability agreements and the early recovery of the value-added tax (VAT).

Agreements on investments

Peru has subscribed 30 agreements on reciprocal promotion and protection of investments and 9 trade agreements containing chapters on investments, which have the highest standards on treatment and protection.

  • Also, the country has subscribed the agreement with the Overseas Private Investment Corporation (OPIC), and the founding treaty of the Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (MIGA).

Dispute settlement

The Constitution recognizes that the investor is able to agreeing on national and international arbitration as mechanism for dispute settlement. This right is also acknowledged in the agreements on investment promotion and chapters on investments in the free trade agreements, guaranteeing the investor the possibility to submitting their cases to international instances like CIADI, the United Nations Commission on International Trade Law (UNCITRAL) or ad-hoc arbitral courts.

  • For the purpose of answering to claims from investors, there exists the System of State Coordination and Response in International Investment Disputes (SICRECI).
  • Also, the legislation on arbitration follows the current international standards.

Legal stability

Legal stability agreements (LSA) guarantee the legal certainty of companies and investors because they have a legally-binding nature. The benefits granted by the LSAs are the following:

To the investor:

  • Stability of the legislation relative to non-discrimination, the Income Tax regime, the regimes about free currency availability and remittance of profits, guarantees, dividends and royalties, and the right to use the most favorable exchange rate available on the market.

To the company receiving the investment:

  • Stability of the regimes about labor hiring, Income Tax and exports promotion used by the company.
  • ProInversión is in charge of processing the subscription of LSAs, whose validity extends for 10 years. The LSAs related to concession agreements are valid during the concession term.
  • The investment commitments for projects under LSAs cannot be less than US$ 10 million in mining and hydrocarbons (for a 2-year term) and US$ 5 million for the remaining sectors (for the same term).
  • Since the inception of the mechanism (1992) until 2015, over 950 LSAs amounting US$ 37 billion in investments have been subscribed.

Early VAT recovery

It is a special regime consisting of the early refund of the Value-Added Tax (VAT) that levied the imports and/or local procurement of new capital goods, intermediate goods, services and construction contracts made during the pre-productive phase of the venture.

  • The mechanism under which the regime operates is an investment contract subscribed between the investor and the State.
  • Investment commitments for the execution of the project under the contract cannot be less than US$ 5 million (there is not any minimum amount for investments in agriculture).
  • Since its implementation in 2007 until 2015, 115 contracts have been subscribed for US$ 28.2 billion in investments.

OPPORTUNITIES

Among its vast production and service activities, Peru offers a number of them that due to their immense potential are attractive investment opportunities, either because they are not sufficiently developed or given the comparative advantages that the country’s geographic characteristics grant them.

Agroindustry

  • Diversity of climates and soils allows the development of top-quality crops and adequate harvest scheduling.
  • The 100,000 ha of land currently devoted to agricultural exports are expected to double as result of the expansion of irrigation projects.
  • Peru is positioning as an exporter of organic and natural produce.
  • More than US$ 5 billion per year in exports of fresh and processed produce to more than 150 countries.

Electricity

  • Great energetic potential: The high availability of water resources and natural gas has enabled the growth of electricity supply.
  • About 50% of power generation is based on natural gas, 48% on hydric sources and 2% on other renewable sources.
  • Other renewable energy sources to be explored such as solar, wind, biomass and geothermic.

Infrastructure

  • One of the pillars of the Government will be the construction of basic infrastructure: transport, education, health and water and sanitation in particular.
  • Regarding transport, Peru has set the goal of increasing connectivity in order to become an exports hub.
  • It is worth mentioning the high component of public-private partnerships (PPP) to be granted through concessions in the coming years: Lines 3 and 4 of the Lima Metro, the Andean Longitudinal Road, the port terminals of Ilo (Moquegua) and Salaverry (La Libertad), the Amazon Waterway and the Lima Peripheral Motorway Ring, among others.

Real estate

  • Low land prices, potential demand and economic stability translate into high profitability.
  • The housing deficit is 25%, which generates an opportunity taking into account that household purchasing power and credit access are on the rise.
  • Also, there is a wide growth margin in the construction of office buildings and shopping centers due to a still low penetration in Lima and the country’s main cities.

Mining

  • First Latin American producer of gold, zinc, lead and tin; second producer of silver, copper and molybdenum.
  • Third global producer of copper, silver, tin and zinc.
  • Large volumes of reserves, including polymetallic deposits.
  • Environmental legal framework aligned with international regulations.
  • Consolidation of the country as a Latin American mining cluster.

Textiles and apparel

  • Textile chain conveniently advanced, from cotton cropping and vicuña and alpaca breeding, to spinning, weaving, designing, dyeing and garment manufacturing.
  • International recognition as “full package” supplier of world’s top brands.
  • A strategic distinguishing factor is the high quality of fine cotton varieties used by local manufacturers: Pima (only grows in Peru, extra-long fiber, one of the finest in the world) and Tangüis (long and resistant fiber).

Tourism

  • Peru is positioned as a world-class destination.
  • 11 attractions declared World Heritage Sites by UNESCO.
  • Peru is one of the few mega-diverse countries, which makes it an unparalleled ecotourism destination.
  • Lima is hailed as the Latin American gastronomic capital.
  • Renowned international hotel chains have established in the country.
  • Growing air connectivity.

PERU AND OECD

In 2008, Peru was admitted by the Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) as adherent to the Declaration on International Investment and Multinational Enterprises. The OECD is comprised by 34 member nations, which are accompanied by 11 observer emerging countries, including Peru.

  • ProInversión is the National Contact Point, in charge of promoting, spreading and implementing OECD’s guidelines on multinational companies.

Country Program

Peru is amongst the first countries to engage with the OECD through a “Country Program”, which consists of revisions of government policies, projects on capabilities development, as well as the country’s participation in OECD committees and its adhesion to certain OECD legal instruments.

  • The inclusion of Peru as a member nation of OECD is a main Government goal.
  • The Country Program is built around five key areas for Peru: economic growth; public governance, anti-corruption and transparency; human capital and productivity; and environment.

Double taxation avoidance

To solve the cases of double international taxation, Peru has signed 7 agreements of double taxation avoidance, which are in accordance with the OECD model: Brazil, Canada, Chile, Mexico, Portugal, South Korea and Switzerland. Additionally, Peru is a member of the Andean Community Double Taxation Treaty.
