WHO WE ARE

THE BEST PARTNER

ProInversión is the Private Investment Promotion Agency of Peru. In order to further the country as an attractive destination for investments, both national and foreign, as well as to enhance the progress of the investment climate, the agency offers information services and orientation. Also, it is in charge of fostering investment in infrastructure projects through the public-private partnership (PPP) scheme.

MISSION AND VISION

ProInversión was created in 2002. Its Governing Board is chaired by the Minister of Economy and Finance, and its members are the ministers of Transport and Communications; Housing, Construction and Sanitation; Energy and Mining; and Agriculture and Irrigation.

Mission

To promote investments carried out by private entities –non-dependent on the Peruvian State– in order to encourage Peru’s competitiveness and its sustainable development, thus improving the well-being of the population.

Vision

To be an agency that investors and the Peruvian population recognize as an effective strategic partner for the advance of investments in Peru.

DUTIES

  1. Implements the national policy of private investment promotion.
  2. Makes policy recommendations on investments.
  3. Guides investors in the management of procedures and necessary activities to materialize their initiatives and projects.
  4. Proceeds and subscribes legal stability agreements and investment contracts.
  5. Performs as the competent national entity in the recording of foreign investments.
  6. Promotes the participation of private investments in public services and public infrastructure works.
  7. Redrafts public investment projects that are not in progress, for the purpose of executing them through public-private partnerships (PPP).

SERVICES

  • Provides investors with information services, free of charge, in the stages of pre-establishment, establishment and development of their investments in Peru.
  • Guides investors during the venture process and facilitates communication with actors relevant for their operations in Peru: potential associates, contractors, business guilds and government entities.
  • Assists in the organization of visits to institutions and/or areas of interest with the provision of tailor-made information.
  • Has a wide network of contacts that includes public and private, national and international entities, as well as the support of the commercial offices and diplomatic missions of Peru abroad.
  • Subscribes agreements on accessing legal stability regimes, tax refunds and early recovery of the Value-Added Tax (VAT).
  • Counsels on the identification of administrative barriers and supports in the solution of the main problems that investors already established in the country face.

PUBLIC-PRIVATE PARTNERSHIPS

Public-private partnerships (PPP) are strategic alliances between the State and private companies aimed to share resources and risks with the objective of developing quality public infrastructure and/or public service projects.

  • ProInversión manages the PPP projects on behalf of the State.
  • Since its creation, ProInversión has awarded PPP projects for over US$ 28 billion in a number of sectors: transportation, telecom, electricity, hydrocarbons, water and sanitation, and tourism, among others.
  • Said projects have contributed to improve and expand infrastructure nationwide, thus increasing the country’s competitiveness.

WORKS FOR TAXES

ProInversión encourages the participation of private companies in the development of public works throughout the Work for Taxes scheme (OxI, by its Spanish acronym). Also, it provides regional and local governments with specialized counseling, for these entities are responsible to conduct processes under the OxI scheme.

  • The scheme consists in the certification of the expenses made by private companies in the execution of public works, in order for them to be accounted as part of their future Income Tax payments.
  • The works to be carried out by means of the scheme correspond to projects prioritized by the diverse levels of Government (National, regional and local), as well as public universities.
  • The scheme has simplified procedures aimed to speed up the works.
  • Since its inception, back in 2008, private companies have finished or carried out 231 OxI projects in sectors that include education, water and sanitation, urbanism and health, among others, which have totaled US$ 824 million and benefited over 11 million Peruvians.
